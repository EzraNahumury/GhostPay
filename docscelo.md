### Why Join?

## **More than a contest.**

Yes, there are rewards. But the real value of Proof of Ship is what you will build, who you will meet, and the reputation you will earn, all of which follow you well beyond the program. Your builder journey does not have to be lonely. It can be **rewarding, exciting, and genuinely fun**.

⚡️ **Build Your onchain Reputation**

Every month you ship, your activity becomes a public credential on the Talent App, unlocking ecosystem opportunities well beyond Proof of Ship.

**🌍 Build Alongside a Global Community**

Connect with fellow builders worldwide, share your progress, get support when you are stuck, and celebrate wins in a community that actually ships things.

🏆 **Earn Monthly Rewards** 

Every month, Celo Public Goods distributes a $**5,000 USDT prize pool** to the Top 50 projects based on impact-driven metrics.

- **Top 10** share 50% of the prize pool, distributed proportionally to score.
    - **Top 3** receive **priority mentor access** (15-min sessions)
    - The **monthly winner** receives user incentives and badges (this perk can only be awarded once per project during Season 2)

**Top 11–50**: Share the remaining 50% of the prize pool, also distributed proportionally to score.

### **Monthly Rhythm**

## **Every week has a purpose.**

Each month is structured around four focused weeks — a thematic framework designed to push your product forward with support at every step. No busywork. Just momentum.

**Week 1 - Scope 💡**

Kick off the month by setting your intentions. What are you building? Share your scope with mentors and get early feedback before you write a single line of code. Top builders from the previous month can book hands-on office hours.

**Week 2- Ship ⚡️**

Build and share. Post your progress in the Proof of Ship group and on your socials. Building in the open is not just good for accountability — it is how you start finding your users before your product is finished.

**Week 3 - Refine 🔍**

Use real data to make your app better. Look at your analytics, collect user feedback, and iterate. This is where good apps become great ones. Work with product mentors to sharpen your user experience before the final leaderboard snapshot.

**Week 4 - Present 📢**


Your app works, it has been tested, and it is better than it was four weeks ago. Now — where does it go next? Define your growth strategy, learn how to pitch your project, and set yourself up for what comes after Proof of Ship.

## ’m new to building. How can I get started?

**⚠️ DISCLAIMER: Never use your wallet with your own funds for development purposes! NEVER. Always use an extra wallet that you send small funds to in case they are needed.**

This page is for everyone who is new to hackathons and builder programs, struggles a bit with how to get started, or just needs a little direction.

### **1. Familiarize yourself with the Proof of Ship**

Builder programs and contests can be daunting, especially for newcomers. Browse through the Proof of Ship page and understand the schedule, challenges, and rules. Join Proof of Ship’s Telegram and feel free to share about your project and ask questions.

### **2. Check your emails and junk regularly**

We'll be sending announcements and check-in emails every week. You can subscribe to our newsletter here. Please ensure you're not missing out on important updates.

### **3. Create Wireframes**

Understand the user flow by creating wireframes; this clarifies your project scope. Start with a basic user flow and gradually add features. Tools like **Figma** or **Miro** are recommended for ease of use.

### **4. Set up your GitHub repository**

When collaborating, start with a shared code base to avoid later merge conflicts. If continuing an existing project, please clone it to clearly showcase what you're building for the Proof of Ship program. For new projects, consider using the **celo-composer**, a hackathon-friendly starter kit that includes MiniPay integration, a hardhat setup for smart contracts, and a frontend ready to deploy on Vercel.

### **5. Outline your Smart Contract functionalities based on the wireframes**

Use your wireframes to guide the development of your smart contracts. This will help you integrate various features and protocols effectively.

### **6. Describe your idea**

Start early by describing your project. This clarifies your vision, helps attract team members, and is useful when seeking assistance. Include this description in the README.md of your GitHub repository and on your hackathon team page.

### **7. Start building!**

Dive into the development process, enjoy the journey, and don't forget to reach out for help! Find all the resources you might need on https://celopedia.celo.org/.

## **Do I need to have any specific qualifications to participate in Proof of Ship?**

Proof of Ship is not a hackathon. It’s a program to support serious builders who want to build real-world solutions and have a real impact. If you love to code, learn, and aim to build something that matters, you are more than welcome to participate in the program.

You can participate as a project owner (as the one who created the project page on talent.app) or a contributor. It’s important to highlight that only the project owner can apply the project in the Proof of Ship page on the talent.app and claim the rewards if the project is in the Top 50 of the given month.

## **Does the entire idea need to be fully working?**

Often, the simpler, the better. **Proof of Ship is currently focused on MiniApps**, so always have in mind the essential features that your idea requires to work and be relevant to your end-users. This being said, your entire idea doesn’t need to be fully implemented; however, the project submitted must be functional, deployed on Celo mainnet, and have onchain activity to be eligible for the rewards.

## **Who will own the IP (Intellectual Property) Rights to the product that I have built?**

The developer/ developers of the software developed will have all rights and own the IP of the product. However, all code needs to be in the public domain (open source) so that it can be tracked on the Leaderboard and evaluated by the Celo Core Co. team.

## How can I present my project properly?

Presenting your project is an expected challenge in the builder's journey. From hackathons to investor meetings, from creating a demo video to presenting it to your users. During Proof of Ship, you have the opportunity to practice your presentation skills by sharing your project with your peers in our Telegram group or by joining our weekly Office Hours. Make sure you:

- **provide a strong context for your idea:** What is the problem that your project aims to solve? Why is this important? How does your project address this problem?
- **competitor analysis and explain why your project would be successful:** What makes it different than other solutions? What is your positioning?
- **understand the market and the user:** What is your Go-To-Market Strategy? Do you already have user feedback?
- **know what it’s needed to reach higher levels**: What do you want to ask the stakeholder that you are presenting your project to? It could be an investment, a partnership, recruiting a team member, or feedback, for example.

## **Do I need to have a product demo?**

During this season (April to July 2026), **the demo video is optional**, but you can provide one in the Project Profile on the talent.app. However, it’s a useful material for different purposes, and it's also a good opportunity to practice your pitch and product presentation. We suggest a 4-minute video with the following content:

- 1 minute into the idea, project, and tech used
- 2-minute walkthrough (recommend to pre-record and then voice over the walkthrough)
- 1-minute challenges, outlook

## **How are rewards calculated?**

Evaluation focuses exclusively on agentic apps development and Celo ecosystem activity. Projects are scored based on these metrics:

- **Onchain activity and impact on Celo mainnet**
    - Fees generated
    - Transaction count
    - Unique active users
- **GitHub development activity**
    - Unique days with contributions
    - Total number of contributions
    - MiniPay-specific dependencies and code usage
- **NPM package downloads**
    - NPM packages downloads published by the repository

All data used to evaluate activity and impact is provided and verified by Talent. Data sources such as smart contracts and GitHub repositories are submitted by builders within the project registration flow.

## **My project is in the Top 50 of the Proof of Ship Leaderboard this month. How can we receive the rewards?**

Congratulations! On the Proof of Ship Leaderboard page, when accessing the project info in the ranking, the project owner (the builder who created the project page on the talent.app) will see the button “Claim Rewards”. This will redirect the project owner to a MiniApp where the project owner will be able to claim the allocated rewards directly on her/his MiniPay wallet. By keeping your rewards on MiniPay, you benefit from a special boost and see your funds grow continuously. If MiniPay is not available in your country, another option will be provided for you to receive your rewards.

Important: Project owners have until the day before the payout starts for the Top 50 of the following Proof of Ship sprint, usually the last day of the month. If not claimed on time, the funds will be reallocated for future builder rewards.
